Who We Are

Nigeria's Federal Asset Management Authority

The Federal Public Assets Maintenance (FPAM) is the operational arm of the Federal Ministry of Housing & Urban Development, mandated to manage, maintain and preserve federal public assets across Nigeria.

Under Presidential Executive Order No. 11, FPAM operates with clear authority to ensure all federal government buildings, housing estates and infrastructure assets are maintained to the highest standards — maximising value for the Nigerian people.

Our Mandate

What We Are
Empowered to Do

FPAM derives its mandate directly from the Federal Government of Nigeria, with powers to act across all asset categories and government agencies.

01
Maintenance & Preservation
Ensure all federal buildings and infrastructure are maintained to standards that preserve their value and extend their serviceable lifespan.
02
Regulatory Compliance
Enforce compliance with building codes, safety regulations and government circulars across all 31 Ministries, Departments and Agencies.
03
Revenue Generation
Optimise commercial returns from federal asset rentals, leases and concessions to supplement government revenue streams.
04
Asset Inventory
Maintain a comprehensive, up-to-date register of all federal assets including buildings, estates, land and infrastructure nationwide.
05
Renovation & Upgrading
Plan and execute renovation projects that modernise federal facilities to meet contemporary operational and living standards.
06
Tenant Welfare
Protect the rights and welfare of federal housing estate residents, ensuring safe, decent and affordable housing conditions.
Presidential Directive

Presidential Executive
Order No. 11

The cornerstone of FPAM's authority, Executive Order No. 11 mandates comprehensive stewardship of all federal public assets, establishes accountability frameworks and empowers the Ministry to enforce maintenance standards across government.

Request Compliance Report
Mandatory Maintenance Standards
All MDAs required to adhere to prescribed maintenance schedules and quality benchmarks for federally-owned assets.
Transparency & Accountability
Regular audits, reports and public disclosures on asset conditions and maintenance expenditure.
Centralised Oversight
FPAM designated as central authority for asset management coordination across all arms of federal government.
Sustainability Mandate
Integration of green building principles and sustainable practices in all maintenance and renovation works.
